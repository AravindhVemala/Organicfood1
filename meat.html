ORGANIC MEAT

What is Organic Meat?
Organic meat comes from animals that are raised in accordance with strict regulations designed to promote animal welfare, environmental health, and consumer well-being. These standards ensure that the meat is produced sustainably and without the use of synthetic chemicals.
Key Standards for Organic Meat Production:
Pasture Access : Organic ruminants like cattle, sheep, and goats must graze for at least 120 days per year on organically managed pastures.
Organic Feed : The diet must be 100% organic, free from growth promotants, antibiotics, GMOs, or animal by-products.
Animal Welfare : Livestock are raised in conditions that accommodate their natural behaviors, such as access to pasture.
Health Benefits of Organic Meat:
Nutritional Value : Organic meat can contain higher levels of omega-3 fatty acids, less cholesterol, and more antioxidants compared to non-organic meat.
Reduced Chemical Exposure : Choosing organic meat reduces exposure to antibiotics, growth hormones, and pesticides.
Environmental Impact:
Soil Health : Organic farming practices support soil health and biodiversity.
Climate Change : Organic meat production can help reduce impacts on climate change by storing more carbon in pasture.
