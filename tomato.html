ORGANIC TOMATOES

Organic Tomatoes: A Fresh Perspective
Cultivation*: Organic tomatoes are grown on inspected farms without synthetic pesticides and fertilizers, and may be grown in soils rather than hydroponically.
Nutritional Profile*: They are a major dietary source of the antioxidant lycopene, linked to many health benefits, including reduced risk of heart disease and cancer.
Vitamins & Minerals*: Tomatoes are a great source of vitamin C, potassium, folate, and vitamin K.
Variety*: Available in colors like red, yellow, orange, green, and purple, with different shapes and flavors.
Water Content*: About 95% water, with the remaining 5% mainly consisting of carbohydrates and fiber.
Health Benefits*: The high antioxidant content, particularly lycopene and beta-carotene, contributes to their health-promoting properties.
Why Organic Matters
Healthier Choice : Organic tomatoes may contain more vitamin C and phenolic content than conventionally grown tomatoes.
Environmental Benefits : Organic farming practices support soil health and reduce the environmental impact of harmful chemicals.
Incorporating Organic Tomatoes
Versatile Use : Can be used in a myriad of dishes, from fresh salads to cooked meals, and are also perfect for making sauces and pastes.
